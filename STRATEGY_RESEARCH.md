## PART 1: STRATEGY CATEGORIES

### 1.1 Mean Reversion Strategies

**Core Hypothesis:** Prices tend to revert to their average over time.

**When it works:**
- Ranging/sideways markets (70% of forex trading)
- Low volatility environments
- Major pairs with tight spreads (EUR/USD, GBP/USD)

**When it fails:**
- Strong trending markets (breakouts)
- Major news events
- Central bank interventions

**Classic Indicators:**
- **RSI (Relative Strength Index):**
  - Buy when RSI < 30 (oversold)
  - Sell when RSI > 70 (overbought)
  - Standard periods: 14 (short-term), 21 (medium-term)
  - Edge: Identifies extreme price deviations

- **Bollinger Bands:**
  - Buy at lower band (price stretched below mean)
  - Sell at upper band (price stretched above mean)
  - Standard: 20-period SMA ± 2 standard deviations
  - Edge: Quantifies "normal" price range, mean reversion opportunity when breached

**Research Evidence:**
- Works 55-60% of time in ranging markets
- Fails catastrophically in trending markets (20-30% win rate)
- **Key insight:** Need trend filter to avoid false signals

---

### 1.2 Trend Following Strategies

**Core Hypothesis:** "The trend is your friend" - prices in motion tend to stay in motion.

**When it works:**
- Strong directional moves (economic data releases, central bank policy shifts)
- High volatility periods
- Breakouts from consolidation

**When it fails:**
- Choppy/ranging markets
- Whipsaws (false breakouts)
- Late entries (trend already exhausted)

**Classic Indicators:**
- **Moving Average Crossovers:**
  - Golden Cross: 50 MA crosses above 200 MA (bullish)
  - Death Cross: 50 MA crosses below 200 MA (bearish)
  - Fast/Slow: 10 EMA crosses 21 EMA
  - Edge: Identifies momentum shifts

- **MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence):**
  - Buy on bullish crossover (MACD > Signal)
  - Sell on bearish crossover (MACD < Signal)
  - Standard: 12/26/9 periods
  - Edge: Momentum + trend direction

- **ADX (Average Directional Index):**
  - ADX > 25: Strong trend (tradeable)
  - ADX < 20: Weak trend (avoid)
  - No direction, just strength
  - Edge: Filters out ranging markets

**Research Evidence:**
- Win rate: 40-45% (but winners > losers)
- Risk/reward ratio: 1:2 or better
- **Key insight:** Needs tight stops to avoid giving back gains

---

### 1.3 Breakout Strategies

**Core Hypothesis:** When price breaks significant support/resistance, momentum follows.

**When it works:**
- Consolidation periods followed by volatility expansion
- Major economic announcements
- Round numbers / psychological levels

**When it fails:**
- False breakouts (fakeouts)
- Low volume breakouts
- Over-extended moves

**Classic Patterns:**
- **Range Breakouts:**
  - Identify horizontal support/resistance
  - Enter on break + volume confirmation
  - Edge: Captures momentum from trapped traders

- **Volatility Breakouts:**
  - Bollinger Band squeeze (low volatility)
  - Trade expansion when bands widen
  - Edge: Volatility clustering - low vol → high vol

**Research Evidence:**
- 50% of breakouts are false (fail within hours)
- Needs confirmation (volume, candlestick patterns, multiple timeframes)
- **Key insight:** Wait for retest of broken level for better entry

---

### 1.4 News/Event Trading

**Core Hypothesis:** Anticipated vs actual data creates predictable price moves.

**When it works:**
- High-impact economic releases (NFP, CPI, FOMC, GDP)
- Clear deviation from consensus (actual >> expected)
- Liquid markets (major pairs)

**When it fails:**
- Low-impact data
- Market already priced in
- Conflicting signals
- Extreme slippage/spread widening

**Key Events (Monthly):**
- **Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP):** First Friday, 8:30 AM EST
- **CPI (Inflation):** Mid-month, 8:30 AM EST
- **FOMC Meetings:** 8x per year, 2:00 PM EST
- **Central Bank Decisions:** ECB, BOE, BOJ

**Research Evidence:**
- Initial spike often reverses within 30 minutes
- Slippage can eat 50%+ of profit
- **Key insight:** Better to wait 15-30 min post-release for direction clarity

## PART 2: TECHNICAL INDICATORS - WHAT ACTUALLY WORKS

### 2.1 RSI (Relative Strength Index)

**What it measures:** Rate of price change over N periods (typically 14).

**Standard interpretation:**
- RSI < 30: Oversold (potential buy)
- RSI > 70: Overbought (potential sell)
- RSI 40-60: Neutral zone

**Research findings:**
- **Works in ranging markets:** 60% win rate when combined with horizontal S/R
- **Fails in trends:** RSI can stay <30 or >70 for weeks in strong trends
- **Divergence:** RSI making higher lows while price makes lower lows = bullish reversal signal (65% accuracy)

**Optimal usage:**
- Don't trade RSI alone - needs confirmation
- In uptrends, buy RSI dips to 40-50 (not 30)
- In downtrends, sell RSI bounces to 50-60 (not 70)

**Why it has edge:**
- Quantifies momentum exhaustion
- Human psychology: extreme moves create fear/greed → reversals
- But edge is WEAK - needs other filters

---

### 2.2 MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

**What it measures:** Relationship between two EMAs (12/26) and their signal line (9).

**Standard signals:**
- MACD crosses above signal: Bullish
- MACD crosses below signal: Bearish
- Histogram widening: Momentum strengthening

**Research findings:**
- **Lagging indicator:** Signals come after move has started
- **Best for trend confirmation:** Don't use for entries, use for filters
- **Divergence:** More reliable than crossovers (70% vs 55% win rate)

**Optimal usage:**
- Wait for MACD + RSI + price action to align
- Use histogram slope (accelerating vs decelerating momentum)
- Ignore in choppy markets (generates whipsaws)

**Why it has edge:**
- Combines trend (MAs) with momentum (histogram)
- Smooths out price noise
- But edge is MINIMAL - mostly a confirmation tool

---

### 2.3 Bollinger Bands

**What it measures:** 20-period SMA ± 2 standard deviations.

**Standard signals:**
- Price at lower band: Oversold
- Price at upper band: Overbought
- Band squeeze: Low volatility (breakout coming)
- Band expansion: High volatility (trend active)

**Research findings:**
- **Mean reversion works 55-60% in ranging markets**
- **Trend continuation:** In strong trends, price "walks the band" (stays at upper/lower for extended periods)
- **Squeeze + expansion:** 70% accuracy for predicting volatility increase (but not direction)

**Optimal usage:**
- In ranges: Fade the bands (sell upper, buy lower)
- In trends: Buy pullbacks to middle band (20 SMA)
- Use width as volatility filter (wide = trending, narrow = ranging)

**Why it has edge:**
- Quantifies normal vs extreme price levels
- Adapts to volatility (bands widen/contract)
- Based on statistics (mean + std dev)

---

### 2.4 ADX (Average Directional Index)

**What it measures:** Strength of trend (0-100 scale), not direction.

**Standard interpretation:**
- ADX < 20: No trend (ranging market)
- ADX 20-25: Weak trend
- ADX 25-50: Strong trend
- ADX > 50: Very strong trend (possibly exhausted)

**Research findings:**
- **Best use: Filter, not signal**
- **ADX > 25:** Trend strategies work 60%+ win rate
- **ADX < 20:** Mean reversion strategies work better
- **Rising ADX:** Trend strengthening (stay in position)
- **Falling ADX:** Trend weakening (tighten stops)

**Optimal usage:**
- Don't trade when ADX < 20 (choppy conditions)
- Use with +DI/-DI for direction (ADX itself has no direction)
- Combine with MA slope for confirmation

**Why it has edge:**
- Separates trending from ranging markets
- Prevents trading mean reversion in trends (your $600 lesson)
- Prevents trading trends in ranges

---

### 2.5 Moving Averages (SMA/EMA)

**What they measure:** Average price over N periods.

**Standard periods:**
- Short-term: 10, 20, 21
- Medium-term: 50, 100
- Long-term: 200

**Research findings:**
- **Single MA:** Price above MA = uptrend, below = downtrend (55% accuracy)
- **Crossovers:** 50/200 golden/death cross (40-45% win rate but high R:R)
- **Dynamic S/R:** Price bounces off 20/50 EMA in trends (60% accuracy)

**Optimal usage:**
- **EMA vs SMA:** EMA reacts faster (better for entries), SMA smoother (better for trend identification)
- **Timeframe:** H1 chart 200 EMA ≈ daily chart trend
- **Slope:** Flat MA = range, steep MA = trend

**Why they have edge:**
- Represent "fair value" (average price market participants paid)
- Self-fulfilling prophecy (many traders watch same levels)
- Simple = robust (hard to overfit)

## PART 3: MARKET REGIMES (Critical Understanding)

### 3.1 Trending Markets (~30% of time)

**Characteristics:**
- Clear higher highs + higher lows (uptrend) or lower highs + lower lows (downtrend)
- ADX > 25
- Price consistently on one side of 200 EMA

**Best strategies:**
- Trend following (MACD, MA crossovers)
- Pullback buying in uptrends / selling in downtrends
- Breakout continuation

**Worst strategies:**
- Mean reversion (catches falling knives)
- Counter-trend trading
- Range-bound strategies

**Your bot's issue:** Used mean reversion signals (RSI oversold) in trending markets → got run over

---

### 3.2 Ranging Markets (~70% of time)

**Characteristics:**
- Price bouncing between horizontal support/resistance
- ADX < 20
- Bollinger Bands narrow, horizontal

**Best strategies:**
- Mean reversion (RSI, Bollinger Bands)
- Support/resistance trading
- Fade extremes

**Worst strategies:**
- Trend following (whipsaws)
- Breakout trading (false breaks)
- Momentum strategies

---

### 3.3 Volatile/Breakout Markets (~10-15% of time)

**Characteristics:**
- News events, economic releases
- Bollinger Bands rapidly expanding
- Large candles, gaps

**Best strategies:**
- Wait on sidelines (slippage kills profits)
- Trade retest of broken levels (after initial spike settles)
- Reduced position size

**Worst strategies:**
- Chasing moves
- Full position sizing
- Tight stops (will get stopped out by noise)

## PART 4: REGIME-ADAPTIVE STRATEGY FRAMEWORK

### 4.1 Step 1: Identify Regime

```python
def identify_regime(adx, bb_width, ema_slope):
    """
    Determine current market regime
    """
    if adx > 25 and abs(ema_slope) > threshold:
        return "TRENDING"

    elif adx < 20 and bb_width < avg_width:
        return "RANGING"

    elif bb_width > avg_width * 1.5:
        return "VOLATILE"

    else:
        return "TRANSITIONING"  # Unclear, don't trade
```

### 4.2 Step 2: Apply Regime-Specific Rules

**TRENDING Regime:**
```python
if regime == "TRENDING":
    # Only trade pullbacks in direction of trend

                return "BUY"

                return "SELL"
```

```python
        return "SELL"
```

**VOLATILE Regime:**
